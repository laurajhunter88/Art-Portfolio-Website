About Me

I have been always a creative person. Growing up I loved singing, acting and dance.
Funnily enough, the only creative things I didn't do were painting and drawing! I did lessons at school but didn't take it for GCSE. Even as a child I was a perfectionist but I obviously didn't have the patience I do now as I used to get frustrated when my art wasn't perfect and then gave up. Two of my brothers are naturally gifted at art and I always used to compare myself to them. I'm very glad I developed some patience as I grew up! But this meant that when I stopped doing at at school, when I was 14 years old, I stopped doing art altogether. I didn't think there was any point as I was rubbish anyway.

Cut to Covid... I was unable to go to singing lessons due to lockdown and as I need to do something creative regularly for my sanity I had a look around on Youtube and found acrylic pouring. I figured you don't need to be able to draw to do that so gave it a go... a loved it! It was super messy but scratched my creative itch.

Then, I got bolder üòè and tried abstract painting in acrylic. Loved that too and, bonus, didn't need to be able to draw! Then as I was looking through Youtube for abstract ideas, I came across a video from Milan Art Institute about their Mastery Program. I was a bit skeptical that they could make me a professional artist in just 1 year but looked at some of their students art as well as the teachers, Dimitra and Eli Milan's art and was amazed. But... that old insecurity said 'but you are really bad a drawing, you can't do that'. I listened to that annoying voice and decided to try the Beginner Program first to see what it was like and if I could improve.

My goodness, did I improve!!! ü§Ø I am blown away by how much I have learnt and progressed in the 8 weeks of the course. Take a look at what I have achieved on the Beginner Program on the Portfolio page.

I am now in the Mastery Program and will be sharing my journey throughout the year long course on Youtube, Instagram, Tiktok and on a weekly blog on this website. If you would like to follow my journey, all of the links to my social media are at the bottom of the page or you can sign up for a weekly newsletter below.
